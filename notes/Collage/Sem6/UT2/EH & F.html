1.A) i) Mobile hacking (3 Marks)

Symptoms of Mobile Hacking:

Mobile hacking refers to activities that seek to compromise mobile devices, such as smartphones, tablets, and even entire mobile networks.

These activities can be motivated by various reasons, including financial gain, protest, information gathering (spying), and the thrill of the challenge12.¬†

Mobile hacking can take many forms, such as phishing, malware attacks, ransomware, cryptojacking, and man-in-the-middle (MitM) attacks2.

Types of Mobile Hacking:

  1. Phishing: Fraudsters send emails or SMS messages designed to steal users‚Äô credentials or sensitive data, such as credit card numbers1.
  2. Malware attacks: Mobile malware is undetected software, such as a malicious app or spyware, created to damage, disrupt or gain illegitimate access to a device2.
  3. Ransomware: A form of malware that threatens to destroy or withhold a victim‚Äôs data or files.
  4. Cryptojacking: A form of malware that uses an organization‚Äôs computing power or individual‚Äôs computer power without their knowledge to mine cryptocurrencies, decreasing a device‚Äôs processing abilities and effectiveness2.
  5. Man-in-the-middle (MitM) attacks: These attacks typically require an employee to be connected to an untrusted or compromised network, such as public Wi-Fi or cellular networks. However, the majority of organizations lack policies prohibiting the use of these networks.
  6. Jailbreaking and rooting: These attacks take advantage of vulnerabilities in the mobile OSs to achieve root access on these devices, enabling an attacker to gain access to more data and cause more damage than with the limited permissions of a standard user4.

1.A) ii) IDS in Network Hacking (3 Marks)

An Intrusion Detection System (IDS) is a security system that monitors network traffic for suspicious activity and alerts when such activity is discovered.

IDSs can be classified into two main types:

  • Network-based IDSs are installed at the perimeter of the network and monitor traffic on LAN, subnets, or important servers.
  • Host-based IDSs are installed on individual host machines and monitor traffic on that specific machin

IDSs can use two main detection methods:

  • Signature-based IDSs detect attacks based on already known malicious instruction sequences used by malware.
  • Anomaly-based IDSs were introduced to detect unknown malware attacks, as new malware is developed rapidly.
    • Machine learning-based methods have a better generalized property compared to signature-based IDSs, as these models can detect a wider range of attacks.

Benefits of IDS:

  1. Detecting intrusions: IDS monitors network traffic for signs of unauthorized access, misuse, or compromise, detecting potential threats in real-time.
  2. Generating alerts: IDS sends alerts to system administrators or security personnel when it detects suspicious or malicious activity, allowing them to take prompt action to mitigate risks.
  3. Insights into network traffic: IDS provides valuable insights into network traffic, helping organizations identify any weaknesses and improve network security.
  4. Monitoring network activity and generating reports: IDS helps organizations meet compliance requirements by monitoring network activity and generating reports, providing greater visibility across the network.
  5. Improve network performance by providing insights into network traffic and identifying any weaknesses that can be addressed to improve security

1.B) iii) Incident Response? (2 Marks)

Computer security incident is any unlawful, unauthorized or unsuitable activity that includes a computer system or computer network.

  • Theft of the trade secrets.
  • Email spam or harassment.
  • Unauthorized or unlawful intrusions into computer system.
  • Denial of service attack.
  • Extortion.
  • Any unlawful action when the evidence of such action may be stored on computer media.
  • Possession or dissemination of child pornography.

The incident response process typically involves the following steps:

  1. Preparation: This involves developing an incident response plan, identifying the team responsible for incident response, and establishing procedures for detecting and reporting incidents.
  2. Detection and Analysis: This involves identifying and analyzing the incident to determine its scope, impact, and cause.
  3. Containment, Eradication, and Recovery: This involves containing the incident to prevent further damage, eradicating the cause of the incident, and restoring systems and data to their normal state.
  4. Post-Incident Activity: This involves reviewing the incident response process to identify areas for improvement and to prevent similar incidents from occurring in the future.

2) i) Challenges in Computer Forensic (5 Marks)

Computer Forensics is a scientific method of investigation and analysis in order to gather evidence from digital devices or computer networks and components which is suitable for presentation in a court of law or legal body.

It involves performing a structured investigation while maintaining a documented chain of evidence to find out exactly what happened on a computer and who was responsible for it.

Technical Challenges

  • Encryption: Encryption can make it difficult to access the data on a device or network, requiring specialized decryption tools and techniques.
  • Data Destruction: Criminals may attempt to destroy digital evidence by wiping or destroying devices, requiring specialized data recovery techniques.
  • Data Storage: The sheer amount of data that can be stored on modern digital devices can make it difficult for forensic investigators to locate relevant information, requiring specialized data carving techniques to extract relevant information.

Legal Challenges

  • Data Tampering: Digital evidence can be tampered with easily, sometimes without any traces, making it difficult to determine the authenticity of the evidence.
  • Legal Limitations: Artificial limitations imposed by constitutional, statutory, and procedural issues can make it challenging to collect and analyze digital evidence.

Other Challenges

  • Increase in PCs and Internet Access: The increasing number of PCs and extensive use of internet access can make it challenging to keep up with the latest devices and technologies.
  • Easy Availability of Hacking Tools: The easy availability of hacking tools can make it difficult to identify and prosecute cybercriminals.
  • Lack of Physical Evidence: The lack of physical evidence can make prosecution difficult, as digital evidence may not be sufficient to prove guilt beyond a reasonable doubt.
  • Large Amount of Storage Space: The large amount of storage space into Terabytes can make it difficult to analyze and locate relevant information.
  • Technological Changes: Any technological changes require an upgrade or changes to solutions, making it challenging to keep up with the latest technologies and tools.

2) ii) Challenges of Mobile Forensic. (5 Marks)

Mobile forensics is a critical area of digital forensics that deals with the recovery of digital evidence from mobile devices.

  1. Rapid growth in mobile technology: New types of mobile devices are developed every week, making it challenging for forensic investigators to keep up with the latest devices and technologies.
  2. Lack of free tools: There are no free tools available that can investigate every single mobile device on the market, making it necessary for forensic investigators to have access to a range of specialized tools.
  3. Expensive and evolving technologies: Mobile forensics technologies, methodologies, and training are becoming more expensive, requiring significant financial resources to sustain professional readiness and perform quality mobile device forensic investigations.
  4. Multiple data locations: Data on mobile devices can be located in different locations, such as on SIM cards, a device's embedded and removable memory, and with the service provider, making it challenging to ensure that all relevant data is collected and analyzed.
  5. Complex data storage systems: Mobile devices use a variety of data storage systems, including proprietary and encrypted systems, making it challenging to extract and analyze data.
  6. Legal and ethical challenges: Mobile forensics investigations often involve legal and ethical challenges, such as obtaining consent to search a device, dealing with data privacy issues, and ensuring that evidence is collected and analyzed in a forensically sound manner.
  7. Training and expertise: Mobile forensics investigations require specialized knowledge and skills, making it essential for investigators to receive appropriate training and have access to adequate budgets to maintain their level of expertise.
  8. Data integrity: Ensuring the integrity of data during the forensic acquisition process can be challenging, particularly when using forensic tools that require a communication vector with the mobile device.
  9. Malicious apps: Malicious apps can pose a significant challenge in mobile forensics investigations, particularly when they are designed to hide in plain sight or to evade detection.
  10. Multiple platforms and operating systems: Mobile devices use a variety of platforms and operating systems, each with its own unique features and challenges, making it necessary for investigators to have a broad range of skills and knowledge.

3) ii) How to analysis a Linux. (5 Marks)

Linux analysis in computer forensics involves the use of various Linux tools and distributions for imaging, data recovery, and analysis of disks and drives. The process typically consists of five major steps: identification, data acquisition, data recovery, analysis, and reporting.

Various shell commands, methods to collect volatile data, the different log files and the information they provide:

  • dmesg: displays the kernel ring buffers, which contains the information about the drivers loaded into kernel.
  • fsck: Is meant for File System Consistency Check. It is a tool to check the consistency of Linux file system and repair.
  • Stat: Displays file or file system status.
  • history: The command history checks and lists the Bash shell commands used. This command helps the users for auditing purposes.
  • mount: The command mount causes mounting of a file system or a device to the directory structure, making it accessible by the system.
