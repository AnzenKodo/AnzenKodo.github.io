  • Z Transforms

Defination: The Z-Transform of a sequence $un$ denoted as $z(Un)$ is defined as

$$Z(Un) = \sum^\infty_{n=-\infty}Unz^{-n}=\bar{u}(z)$$

Where $\bar{u}(z)$ is the Z-Transform of $un$ & $Z$ is a complex number.

Z-Transform exists only when the infinite series in (1) is convergent.

The sequence Un is called the inverse Z-Transform of $\bar{u}(z)$ & is written as $Z^{-1}[\bar{u}(z)] = un$

Examples

$$ Z(4^n) = {z \over z-4} $$ $$ z(6^n) = {z \over z-6} $$ $$ z(a^n) = {z \over z-a} $$ $$ z^{-z}\bigg({z \over z-5}\bigg)=(5)^n $$

Show that $Z[a^n] = {z \over z-a}, n \geq 0$

$$ Z(Un) = \sum^\infty{n=0} Unz^{-n}\ = \sum^\infty{n=0} a^nz^{-n}\ = \sum^\infty_{n=0}({a\over z})^z + ({a \over z})^1 + ... \ = {1 \over a - {a \over z}}\

\boxed{Z(a^n) = {z \over z-a}} $$
